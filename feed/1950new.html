India in the 1950s

A New Beginning

The 1950s was a decade of hope, resilience, and foundation-building for newly independent India. After gaining freedom from British rule in 1947, the nation focused on establishing democratic institutions and planning for economic development.

Historical Overview

Democratic Foundations

In 1950, India adopted its constitution and became a republic. The first general elections were held in 1951-52, establishing the world's largest democracy, with Jawaharlal Nehru becoming India's first elected Prime Minister.

Political Development

Five-Year Plans

India adopted socialist-inspired economic policies focused on state-led industrialization. The First Five-Year Plan (1951-56) focused on agriculture, while the Second Five-Year Plan (1956-61) emphasized industrialization.
