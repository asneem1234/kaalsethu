Music Industry
_
‚ñ°
X

The 1990s saw the rise of music cassettes and CDs in India, with companies like T-Series and Venus dominating the market. Music videos on MTV India became immensely popular, and Indipop emerged as a genre with artists like Alisha Chinai, Baba Sehgal, and Colonial Cousins.

The era of 90s Bollywood music is often considered the golden age with composers like A.R. Rahman, Nadeem-Shravan, and Jatin-Lalit creating memorable melodies.

Fashion Icons
_
‚ñ°
X

The 1990s saw Bollywood stars becoming major fashion influencers. Madhuri Dixit's outfits were copied nationwide, while Shah Rukh Khan's cool boy looks from "Kuch Kuch Hota Hai" inspired young men.

International brands began entering India after economic liberalization, and local markets filled with "imported" clothes and accessories. The fusion of Western and traditional Indian styles became popular among the youth.

Indian Youth Culture
_
‚ñ°
X

The 1990s marked a cultural shift as India opened up to global influences. Youth culture was characterized by a blend of traditional values and Western trends, particularly in urban areas.

Young Indians were captivated by Channel V and MTV, while also participating in traditional celebrations. The concept of "hanging out" at newly opened malls and fast-food joints became popular in metropolitan cities.
